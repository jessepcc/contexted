## Design Context

### Users
Contexted is for frequent AI users who are tech savvy and already comfortable with products like ChatGPT and Claude. They are not looking for a conventional dating-app experience; they are participating in a thoughtful, curiosity-driven experiment about whether AI memory and written context can surface better interpersonal compatibility than photos and swiping. The product should respect their intelligence, feel culturally fluent in AI, and reduce the self-consciousness and performative insecurity common in mainstream dating apps.

### Brand Personality
Warm, witty, intimate.

The interface should feel like an experiment with taste: emotionally intelligent, slightly provocative, and self-aware without becoming ironic or cold. It should create confidence and curiosity rather than pressure or comparison. The product voice should feel literate, tech-native, and human, with enough personality to be memorable while still feeling trustworthy around privacy and emotional vulnerability.

### Aesthetic Direction
Default to the warm light aesthetic already present in the product: cream backgrounds, editorial spacing, soft depth, terracotta accents, restrained violet highlights, and a premium text-first presentation. Preserve the "words over faces" positioning and the contrast between AI/system motifs and human warmth. Reference direction should feel closer to an intentional experimental product or cultural prototype than a mass-market dating app.

Anti-references:
- Not Tinder-like
- Not swipey, gamified, or insecurity-inducing
- Not built around glossy desirability signaling or competitive profile aesthetics

### Design Principles
1. Words lead, visuals support. Prioritize writing, memory excerpts, and emotional texture over profile-like presentation.
2. Reduce insecurity by design. Avoid patterns that invite comparison, ranking, vanity metrics, or performative self-optimization.
3. Make the experiment feel intentional. Lean into the AI-native premise with clear product metaphors, but keep the experience warm and human.
4. Default to warm calm confidence. Use the warm light palette, soft motion, and premium spacing to create emotional safety and trust.
5. Design for fluent users, not first-time tourists. Assume a tech-savvy audience and keep interactions crisp, culturally aware, and free of unnecessary explanation.
